Optimal Skip Filter
Skip days with: Volatile spikes + NASDAQ drops >1.5% + VIX spikes
Result: WR 73.7% | PF 2.85 | MaxDD -0.21 (1,523 trades)
This filter removes ~10% of trading days but improves risk-adjusted returns significantly.
Methodology: Walk-forward backtest using 1-hour bars (2Y) and daily bars (3Y) from yfinance. No look-ahead bias -- each day uses only the previous close as baseline. Re-entry protection: after a stop-loss, the same direction is blocked for the remainder of that day. Parameters optimized via grid sweep of 49 combinations (7 window widths x 7 stop extensions).

Signal Types

B BUY Signal

Price drops to the Lower Bound (-0.3% from baseline). The system expects mean-reversion back to baseline. You enter a LONG position.

S SELL Signal

Price rises to the Upper Bound (+0.3% from baseline). The system expects mean-reversion back down. You enter a SHORT position.

TP Take Profit

Price reverts back to Baseline. The trade is a WIN. Close position and collect profit. Happens ~73% of the time.

SL Stop Loss

Price keeps moving against you, hitting the Stop Loss (0.8% beyond entry). The trade is a LOSS. Direction is blocked for the rest of the day.

Typical Sunday Session Timeline

08:00 -- Market Open
System fetches Friday's close as baseline, computes window bounds, checks event calendar, VIX level, and news feeds.
08:00-09:00 -- Opening Volatility
Weekend gaps cause initial volatility. Price often overshoots. First signals typically appear in this window.
09:00-15:00 -- Core Trading
Most signals occur here. If price hits a bound, expect mean-reversion. US market opens at 16:30 IL time -- watch for correlation shifts.
15:00-17:30 -- Late Session
US pre-market activity. News-driven moves more likely. Any open positions close at EOD if not already resolved.
17:30 -- Market Close
All positions closed. Blocked directions reset. System waits for next day.

Key Insight
The overnight gap size (abs_gap_pct) accounts for 40% of the prediction. Large gaps predict bad mean-reversion days. The ML model learned to skip those days automatically.

Why Not Neural Networks?

1. Data Size
~2,900 daily bars is too small. Neural networks need 10,000+ samples to generalize.
2. Structural Edge
USD/ILS mean-reverts due to BoI intervention -- a fixed market property, not a learnable pattern.
3. Overfitting Risk
Tree models (RF, XGBoost) handle small datasets better and are fully interpretable.
4. Signal-to-Noise
Currency data is extremely noisy. NNs amplify noise with insufficient data.
